GTM and competitive intelligence for agentic AI governance.

Inversed built Worldcoin's aMPC privacy system underpinning World ID at scale. Now launching Threshold — a governance proxy that vaults AI agent credentials, enforces deterministic access boundaries, and logs every action in an audit trail the agent can't touch. When you hand an agent your credentials and a Turing-complete execution environment, the agent is you — same identity, same permissions, same blast radius. Existing governance addresses the surface. My work here is building the case and the tooling for governance that moves beneath the LLM, before the action, not after.

My job was to build narrative frameworks that made all three land simultaneously, across audiences, across content formats, across ecosystem teams doing the same translation for their own work.

Community built on conviction, not incentives

Community as a discipline at the launch of restaking.

Restaking became one of the most influential architectural ideas in crypto in 2023 and 2024: a primitive that allowed stakers to extend cryptoeconomic security to other protocols, opening new design space for decentralized infrastructure. I joined to build the community function from scratch at the moment the category was being defined.

The community coherence problem

Restaking was a new primitive and the community it needed did not exist yet. Builders, researchers, operators, and restakers all had to understand what was being built before they could believe in it. My job was to create coherence at the moment the category was being defined, when very few outside the protocol understood what cryptoeconomic security extensibility meant. The community that formed was built with understanding, not incentives.

What I built
  • Defined community as a discipline from the ground up, establishing the core workstreams: sensemaking and analytics, growth, moderation and support, events and programs. These gave the function shape, accountability, and an operating rhythm it did not have before
  • Designed and led a community retro distribution of 6M tokens to contributor wallets: a complex, high-stakes operation carrying significant reputational and operational weight
  • Co-directed and executive produced Watch: Unlocking Human Coordination ↗ about the protocol's core ethos: a long-form narrative artifact for a technical concept that required emotional resonance, not just explanation
  • Ran point on all community workstreams across both stakedrop launches and the Token Generation Event (TGE)
  • Built the ambassador and fellowship program, creating a distributed layer of educated, credible community leadership
  • Contributed to brand strategy for both the Foundation and Labs arms of the organization
// why this matters

The architecture was technical and novel. The audience was builders, researchers and restakers. The growth lever was community conviction before mainstream product-market fit. People needed to really comprehend the extensibility of ethereum's cryptoeconomic security to other proof of stake systems. I built that playbook from first principles.
